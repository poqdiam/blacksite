Privacy Notice

Effective date: 2026-03-09  ·  Last reviewed: 2026-03-09  ·  Next review: 2027-03-09

{# ── 1. Who We Are ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

1. Who We Are and What This Platform Is

BLACKSITE is a Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform designed for security practitioners managing compliance programs under frameworks such as NIST SP 800-53, FedRAMP, FISMA, CMMC, HIPAA, and related standards. The platform is operated by a small development team and serves federal, state, local, tribal, and commercial organizations.

This notice applies to all individuals who access BLACKSITE, including registered users, organizational administrators, and demo visitors. It describes how we collect, process, store, and share personal data.

{# ── 2. Personal Data We Collect ──────────────────────────────────────────── #}

2. Personal Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal information when you use the platform.

Account Data

Data Element Description Required
Full name Display name for the account Yes
Email address Account identification and notifications Yes
Username Unique identifier used for login Yes
Password hash Bcrypt hash only; plaintext is never stored Yes
Role and access level Admin, staff, or read-only designation Yes
Last login timestamp Date and time of most recent successful authentication Automatic
IP address Collected on login events for audit trail and anomaly detection Automatic

Activity and Audit Data

BLACKSITE maintains an immutable audit log. The following events are recorded with user, timestamp, IP address, and action details: login and logout events; record creation, modification, and deletion; administrative actions; AI assistant queries; compliance workflow events.

Session and Visitor Data

A session cookie (required for authentication) is set on login. It is a server-side token containing no personal data client-side. For demo or unauthenticated visitors, the IP address and browser user-agent string may be collected for aggregate analytics.

Compliance Workflow Data

Users may enter personal data about other individuals (e.g., ISSO names, system owner names, contractor information) as part of compliance documentation. Users are responsible for ensuring they have appropriate authority to enter such data.

{# ── 3. How We Use It ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

3. How We Use Your Data

Data Purpose
Account data Authentication, authorization, account management
Audit log Security monitoring, compliance evidence, incident investigation
IP address (authenticated) Audit trail, anomalous login detection
IP address (visitor/demo) Demo analytics (country-level aggregate only)

BLACKSITE does not use personal data for advertising, profiling, resale, or any purpose outside of platform operations.

{# ── 4. Third-Party Processors ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

4. Third-Party Processors

Groq API — AI Compliance Assistant

When a user submits a query to the BLACKSITE AI compliance assistant, the query text is transmitted to Groq, Inc. for processing via the llama-3.3-70b-versatile model.

  • What is sent: The user's query text and a system prompt defining the assistant's role.
  • What is NOT sent: Usernames, email addresses, account identifiers, SSP content, or any platform data beyond the explicit query.
  • Policy: Users must not include personally identifiable information (PII) or protected health information (PHI) in AI assistant queries.
  • Groq Privacy Policy

ip-api.com — Geo-IP Lookup (Demo Analytics Only)

Visitor IP addresses may be sent to ip-api.com to determine the approximate geographic origin (country/region) for demo visitor analytics.

  • What is sent: Visitor IP address only.
  • What is NOT sent: Account data, credentials, or session identifiers.
  • Note: This service is used only for unauthenticated demo visitor tracking. Authenticated user IPs are not sent to this service.

{# ── Do Not Sell ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information

BLACKSITE does not sell, rent, or share personal information with third parties for their own marketing purposes. We do not sell consumer personal information as defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) or any other applicable law. No opt-out action is required — this platform has never engaged in the sale or sharing of personal data for commercial purposes.

{# ── 7. California Residents ──────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

7. California Residents — CCPA/CPRA Rights

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: You may request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell.
  • Right to Delete: You may request deletion of personal information we hold about you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Correct: You may request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: We do not sell or share personal information. No opt-out action is required.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any CCPA rights.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information: We do not use sensitive personal information beyond the purposes specified in this notice.

To submit a CCPA request, contact the platform administrator via My Data & Privacy or the contact information in Section 10 below. We will verify your identity before processing requests.

{# ── 8. GDPR / EU Residents ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

8. GDPR and EU / EEA Residents

For users located in the European Union or European Economic Area, BLACKSITE processes personal data on the following legal bases under GDPR Article 6:

  • Article 6(1)(b) — Contractual necessity: Account data, session data, and audit logs are necessary to deliver the platform service.
  • Article 6(1)(f) — Legitimate interests: Security monitoring and audit logging are in the legitimate interest of the operator and all users to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Article 6(1)(c) — Legal obligation: Retention of audit logs for 3 years is required for federally connected systems under FISMA.

EU residents have the rights described in Section 6, as well as the right to lodge a complaint with your national supervisory authority. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available on request for enterprise customers. Contact the administrator for details.

{# ── 9. Children's Privacy ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

9. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

BLACKSITE is a professional compliance management platform intended exclusively for use by authorized personnel within organizational security programs. It is not directed at, marketed to, or designed for use by persons under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If it is discovered that a minor has registered an account, that account will be deleted upon discovery and any associated data removed in accordance with applicable law (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act — COPPA, 15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506).

{# ── 10. Cookies and Tracking ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

10. Cookies and Tracking

  • Session cookies: Required for maintaining login state. These are server-side session tokens. No personal data is stored client-side in the cookie itself.
  • Preference cookies: Non-identifying user interface preferences (theme, font size) may be stored as cookies.
  • No third-party tracking cookies: No analytics platforms (Google Analytics, etc.) are embedded in the platform.
  • No persistent fingerprinting: The platform does not implement browser fingerprinting or cross-session tracking beyond the server-side session.
